ST-20

Security Tokens Standard

We are bringing together legal professionals, businesses and developers to create and refine a standard for security tokens.

Polymath’s ST20 token standard simplifies the process of creating and investing in security tokens. Our simple, transparent ST20 token standard is open-source and has KYC (Know Your Customer) built-in. And we want YOU to join the network first.

Polymath is upgrading the existing capital markets infrastructure to take advantage of blockchain technology’s benefits: seamless capital formation; instantaneous settlement of unique, unforgeable assets; negligible trading fees; and countless others.

We believe there needs to be a standard for security tokens that utilize these benefits and satisfies regulations. With a standard in place, security token issuers, investors, exchanges, wallets, custody providers, and regulators can become comfortable with this technology, interoperability is much easier to achieve, and adoption can be widespread. 

The standard we have built at Polymath is ST-20. ST-20 is an extension of ERC-20 that introduces the ability to restrict transfers of blockchain tokens. ERC-20 tokens do not have any transfer restrictions and therefore can be freely traded by anyone. We provides you with a full user management functions that results in faster development, faster revenue, user and the ability to serve your users engaging efficiently.

This is fine for so-called utility tokens. When dealing with securities however, all securities holders must be KYC/AML verified, and there are many additional restrictions on the distribution and trading of securities. ST-20 is the solution to this problem. It allows security token issuers to maintain regulatory compliance through transfer restrictions. And beneficially, it is backwards compatible with ERC-20 making it interoperable with much of the existing blockchain infrastructure available today.

In the next step for mass adoption, ST-20 has been formalized as an Ethereum Request for Comment to be merged with the Ethereum codebase, titled ERC1400. ERC-1400 - The Security Token Standard has received incredible community support and feedback, and we are looking forward to its continued evolution.
